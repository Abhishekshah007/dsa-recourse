<h1>DSA and AI Resources</h1>
<p>Welcome to the <strong>DSA and AI Resources</strong> repository! This repository is a curated collection of books to help you master <strong>Data Structures</strong>, <strong>Algorithms</strong>, <strong>Discrete Mathematics</strong>, and <strong>Artificial Intelligence</strong>. The materials are organized to guide you from beginner-friendly concepts to advanced topics.</p>
<hr>
<h2>Directory Structure</h2>
<ul>
    <li><strong>DSA-books</strong>: A collection of resources for Data Structures and Algorithms.</li>
    <li><strong>Maths-book</strong>: A reference for Discrete Mathematics.</li>
    <li><strong>AI-books</strong>: A book on Artificial Intelligence.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>How to Begin</h2>
<p>If you&apos;re starting your journey, here&rsquo;s the recommended order to follow for <strong>Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA)</strong> books, organized from beginner-friendly to advanced.</p>
<h3>1. Beginner-Friendly Books</h3>
<ul>
    <li>
        <p><strong>Grokking Algorithms by Aditya Bhargava</strong><br>A visually engaging book with easy-to-understand explanations and illustrations. Perfect for beginners.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <p><strong>JavaScript Data Structures and Algorithm Book</strong><br>Focused on JavaScript implementations, this book is great for practicing algorithms while coding.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <p><strong>Data Structures and Algorithm Made Easy by Narasimha Karumanchi</strong><br>A simplified approach to understanding core concepts, with a practical coding focus.</p>
    </li>
</ul>
<h3>2. Intermediate Level</h3>
<ul>
    <li>
        <p><strong>Algorithm Design Manual (3rd Edition) by Steven Skiena</strong><br>Provides practical problem-solving tips and real-world examples of algorithm design.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <p><strong>Algorithms by Sanjoy Dasgupta, Christos Papadimitriou, and Umesh Vazirani</strong><br>Combines theoretical and practical aspects of algorithms in a concise format.</p>
    </li>
</ul>
<h3>3. Advanced Level</h3>
<ul>
    <li>
        <p><strong>Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)</strong><br>A comprehensive and authoritative guide to algorithms. Recommended for those seeking an in-depth understanding.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <p><strong>Instructor Manual for CLRS (3rd Edition)</strong><br>A supplementary resource with solutions and additional exercises from the CLRS textbook.</p>
    </li>
    <li>
        <p><strong>The Art of Computer Programming - Vol 1 by Donald Knuth</strong><br>A legendary book in computer science. Ideal for those with a strong theoretical interest in programming and algorithms.</p>
    </li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>Discrete Mathematics</h2>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications (8th Edition) by Kenneth H. Rosen</strong><br>This book is highly recommended to build a strong foundation in mathematical concepts used in computer science.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>Artificial Intelligence</h2>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach (3rd Edition) by Stuart Russell and Peter Norvig</strong><br>A leading textbook for learning AI concepts, ideal for both beginners and advanced learners.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>How to Use These Resources</h2>
<ul>
    <li>Start with beginner-friendly books to build a solid foundation.</li>
    <li>Progress to intermediate and advanced books as your confidence grows.</li>
    <li>Refer to the <strong>Discrete Mathematics</strong> book for theoretical clarity and problem-solving skills.</li>
    <li>Explore the <strong>AI book</strong> after gaining proficiency in DSA and discrete math.</li>
</ul>
